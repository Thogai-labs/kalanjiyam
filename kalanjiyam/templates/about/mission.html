Mission

Making Siddha Knowledge Systems radically accessible through digital preservation.

{# MAIN CARD #}

Kalanjiyam's mission is to preserve and make accessible the ancient Siddha Knowledge Systems tradition through collaborative digital preservation. Specifically, we have three goals:

{# GOALS #}
  1. Create a comprehensive digital archive of traditional Siddha literature through collaborative proofing.
  2. Preserve this archive in an open, accessible format for future generations.
  3. Provide collaborative tools for researchers, practitioners, and community members.

A complete archive

The Siddha corpus is vast. Thousands of manuscripts exist across medicine, alchemy, yoga, and spiritual knowledge. While many are near-duplicates, they collectively represent a deep and rich tradition.

Only a fraction exist as published books, even fewer as digital scans, and fewer still as clean machine-readable text. This fragmentation makes preservation difficult.

Existing archives have made sincere efforts, and we deeply respect their work. However, full-scale preservation requires significantly more coordination and engineering effort.

We aim to reduce the cost of digitization through engineering systems that make large-scale collaborative proofing possible.

An open format

A complete archive is meaningless if it is not accessible. We ensure that texts remain open, readable, and reusable.

Our platform publishes content in open formats with permissive licensing and readable design across devices.

We are working toward multilingual support so knowledge can be accessed in native languages globally.

Collaborative preservation tools

Our proofing system enables community-driven preservation of Siddha texts with accuracy and version control.

Contributors can transcribe and verify manuscripts through structured workflows that improve data quality.

Researchers gain access to revision history and verified texts, ensuring authenticity is preserved.

The broader community can contribute through transcription, translation, and knowledge sharing.
